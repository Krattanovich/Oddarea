www.eosturf.io

Turf is a site where you can buy a piece of the world map. Your piece of land (Turf) is saved on the EOS block chain. payment is made with scatter (www.get-scattar.com)


What Turf brings the table is a new way of interact with internet. The application it self uses the world map and is perfectly fine to navigate around if you are going somwhere. With the Turf purchased buy other users you will get a direct view what is going on around the world. Maybe you will find new friends, a restaurant offer or a nightcub event you never would have found in a classic search engine.

The point is Turf makes internet more connected to our physical world, you will find what you want instead of figure out what you want and then put a search word in your classic search engine.

The UX design process started with playing around with Google maps and getting involved as a city  guide at Google maps. this gave a good benchmark of what a navigation application does.


Next step was putting up pros and cons regarding the application.


Next step had to be to speak with friends and colleagues when they use Google maps and what their thoughts are about tue usage of the app.


Next step was finding a way be able to integrate block chain technology to save information and ownership of areas of the map. and what information to store an what not to store at the block chain.


Next step was to define the smart contracts to use for the pruchases. To start out using Scatter for payment with EOS tokens for an area of the map. Scatter aswell work as a login function witch allows the user to be logged in to the website as long as their scatter app is running. This is a smooth way of skipping the boring sign up process with account name, password, email, email verification, forgetting password etc.


Next step is to figure out what makes the user involved as fast as possible. The mindset here has been to get straight in to the core. Much like Google maps gets you straight to the core of the product on enter. And since scatter is used this takes the user straight into the core able to buy administrate their turf.


Next step we wanted to figure out the feel of the website, here we have sent the test site to people in different ages and sex to recieve feedback. Also making users test the website asking them to preforem different tasks while watching them, taking notes what their first instinct is, what questions do they ask, how many clicks does it take to preform the task. And what do they have to say of the feeling of the website.
